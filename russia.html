Local Food and Culture

Traditional Russian Cuisine
Russian food is hearty and comforting, designed to keep people warm through long winters. Popular dishes include borscht (beet soup), pelmeni (dumplings), and blini (thin pancakes).
Beverages
Tea is a central part of Russian life, often served with sugar, jam, or pastries. Kvass and mors are traditional local drinks made from bread or berries.
Festivals and Traditions
Russia celebrates unique festivals like Maslenitsa (Butter Week), where people eat pancakes, sing folk songs, and welcome the coming of spring.

Daily Life and Customs
Russians value hospitality, family gatherings, and deep conversations over meals. Sharing food and tea is seen as a sign of friendship and respect.
